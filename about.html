Our Mission

Princeton University Science Olympiad seeks to translate the collective wealth of student experience and institutional resources at Princeton into providing teams of all backgrounds with the best invitational experience possible. This tournament is led and organized entirely by students at Princeton who have medaled at the highest levels of competition, and have ran events for regional, state and invitational tournaments across the country. Our team knows exactly what goes into an outstanding Science Olympiad tournament experience, and on Saturday February 10th, 2018, we will be hosting the second annual Princeton University Science Olympiad Invitational Tournament for Division C (high school).

What We Offer

All 23 Division C events will run per the national event rules and the competition schedule will closely resemble that of the National Tournament, allowing for a challenging and representative experience. Coaches will not be required to volunteer or run any events, and thus are free to supervise their teams. Our invitational tournament provides teams with rigorous preparation for regional and state tournaments, as well as the national tournament. Last year, nearly half of the events were ran by national medalists, and all events were ran by students with a track record of medaling at states and top-tier invitationals. All exams and labs undergo multiple revisions by our executive board for consistency, appropriate difficulty spread and content relevance. This year, we once again offer a fun and challenging competition experience suitable for teams of all levels and backgrounds. Following the tournament, all teams will receive copies of the tests and solutions. Full tests and solutions from last year may be found in the archive.

History

Princeton University Science Olympiad was founded in fall 2016 by David Fan ('19) and Edison Lee ('19) with the administrative support of the School of Engineering and Office of Environmental Health and Safety. The very first Princeton University Invitational hosted 36 teams from four states on February 4th, 2017. In its second year, Princeton University Science Olympiad looks forward to expanding the tournament to 45 teams, waiving registration fees, as well as continuing to perfect all aspects of the competition experience.
